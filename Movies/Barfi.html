Barfi!

film by Anurag Basu

Set in 1970s India, "Barfi." follows Barfi, a hearing and speech-impaired young man who falls for Shruti. Despite her love for him, societal pressures lead Shruti to marry someone considered "normal." Years later, Barfi, now in love with Jhilmil, seeks her as he eludes the police. When Shruti realizes Jhilmil is autistic, she understands true love transcends societal norms. In their pursuit, Shruti rediscovers her love for Barfi.

Characters

Barfi

Ranbir Kapoor

Barfi is a charming and mischievous young man who communicates through gestures and expressions rather than words. He's like that friend who always brings a smile to your face, despite facing challenges in life. Throughout the movie, we see Barfi's playful and adventurous spirit as he navigates through life with a sense of curiosity and resilience. Despite being deaf and mute, Barfi finds joy in the simple pleasures of life and touches the hearts of everyone he meets. His character reminds us that communication goes beyond words and that love knows no boundaries.

Shruti Ghosh

Ileana D'Cruz

Shruti is a beautiful and kind-hearted woman who falls in love with Barfi but struggles to choose between her heart and societal expectations. She's like that person torn between following their dreams and fulfilling their obligations. Throughout the movie, we witness Shruti's journey of self-discovery as she learns to listen to her heart and embrace love in all its forms. Despite the challenges she faces, Shruti's character embodies the courage to follow one's heart and the resilience to overcome obstacles in pursuit of happiness.

Jhilmil Chatterjee

Priyanka Chopra

Jhilmil is a young woman with autism who forms a deep connection with Barfi and teaches him the true meaning of love and acceptance. She's like that friend who sees the beauty in everything and reminds us to appreciate the little things in life. Despite facing discrimination and misunderstanding from society, Jhilmil's innocence and purity of heart shine through, touching the lives of everyone around her. Her character reminds us that love transcends differences and that true happiness lies in embracing each other's uniqueness.
